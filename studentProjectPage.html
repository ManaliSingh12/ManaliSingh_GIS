Choropleth Map

Visualizing global status regarding access to safely managed drinking water services to assess progress toward equitable infrastructure solutions.

GIS Analysis
Cartography
Sustainability
2026

Project Overview

This map visualizes Sustainable Development Goal (SDG) Goal 6 access to safely managed drinking water services, specifically focusing on SDG Target 6.1: achieving universal and equitable access to safe and affordable drinking water for all by 2030.

The visual framework displays the proportion of each country's population using safely managed drinking water services broken down by urban/rural classification. By analyzing these spatial patterns, the project highlights significant regional inequalities and uncovers critical gaps in global sustainability data tracking.

Methods

The dataset mapping workflow was completed using professional vector visualization and composition techniques in QGIS:

  • Styling Vector Layers with Graduated Symbology: Classified the continuous percentage data variable tracking water access across distinct statistical ranges.
  • Adding and Formatting Map Elements: Inserted and customized structured cartographic features, including layout titles, label text containers, and map boundaries.
  • Map Labels & Callouts: Formatted prominent callouts on specific highly-developed nations to highlight exceptional coverage parameters directly on the map plane.
  • QGIS Print Layout Architecture: Engineered a formal output framework featuring scale balancing, precise boundary framing, and clear element separation at standard delivery resolutions.

Results & Findings

Countries are shaded across six data categories ranging from 10–28% coverage up to 100% total coverage. A unique bright green category is applied to bring immediate visual emphasis to nations that have achieved 100% water safety, while missing statistics are cleanly categorized in grey.

The visualization establishes a stark geographic contrast: high-access territories such as Iceland, France, Hungary, Monaco, Gibraltar, San Marino, Kuwait, Singapore, Hong Kong, and New Zealand stand out alongside large grey and light-blue clusters spanning across sub-Saharan Africa and regions of Asia where data remains absent or municipal infrastructure remains underdeveloped.

Reflection

A choropleth presentation style proves exceptionally beneficial for evaluating statistical trends across global administrative frameworks:

  • The custom color gradients map macro spatial trends instantly without forcing the user to decode extensive quantitative tables.
  • Enables immediate comparative evaluation between divergent hemispheres over extended spatial extents.
  • Provides an ideal cartographic medium for processing normalized, percentage-based proportional information.
